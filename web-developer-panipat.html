Hiring a Web Developer in Panipat - A Complete Checklist

A practical hiring checklist for business owners who want a developer that ships clean, reliable, maintainable work.

Introduction

Hiring a web developer in Panipat is straightforward if you know what to look for. Ask the wrong questions and you end up with a site that looks fine but breaks constantly, loads slowly, or needs a developer every time you want to change a line of text. Ask the right questions and you end up with a site that works, that you can manage, and that grows with your business.

Pair this checklist with our website development explainer and our article on good website design in 2025 so you evaluate both code quality and user experience together.

The pre-hire checklist

Can you show me live websites you have built?

Not mockups. Not screenshots. Live URLs you can click and test yourself on mobile and desktop. If they hesitate, move on.

Who specifically will build my site?

Agencies sometimes sell you on senior work then hand the project to a junior. Know exactly who is touching your code.

What platform will you build on and why?

The answer should match your needs, not just whatever they know best. Ask what the pros and cons are for your specific project.

Will I be able to update content myself after launch?

If the answer is no or vague, you will be paying for every small update forever. Insist on a CMS with a training handover.

What is included in post-launch support?

Bugs found after launch. Security updates. Hosting issues. What is covered and for how long, get this in writing.

What good web development looks like in practice

Clean, commented code. Mobile-first build. Fast load times under 3 seconds. SSL and security basics. Google Analytics installed. A CMS you can actually use. A handover session where everything is explained.

Red flags when hiring a web developer

No contract or vague scope of work. Unwilling to share previous client contacts. Promises unrealistically fast timelines. Cannot explain their technology choices in plain language. No post-launch support included.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a web designer and a web developer?

Designers focus on visual appearance and user experience. Developers bring those designs to life with code. At DWD we do both.

Can you fix bugs on my existing website?

Yes. We offer one-off bug fixing and ongoing maintenance retainers for businesses that need reliable technical support.

Do you build custom web applications?

Yes. We build custom web apps including booking systems, customer portals, dashboards, and more.

Do you offer hourly or project-based rates?

Both. Small fixes are handled hourly. Full projects are quoted at a fixed price so there are no surprises.

How do I know if my current developer is doing a good job?

Run your site through Google PageSpeed Insights and GTmetrix, both free. If scores are poor and your developer has no explanation, that is your answer.
